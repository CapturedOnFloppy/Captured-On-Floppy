Parts for sale and some aftermarket replacements
for Sony Mavica cameras, though availability depends heavily on the
model, part, and the current market. Here’s a breakdown of what you can expect:

1. Commonly Available Parts ( short reference)
      2025 Captured On Floppy — All rights reserved.

  • Batteries:
    • Aftermarket NP-F330/F550 (“L” series) and NP-FM50 (“M” series) InfoLithium batteries are widely available.
    • Many are third-party/aftermarket, some are NOS (new old stock) Sony.
  • Battery Chargers:
    • Aftermarket chargers and USB adapters are common for both L and M series.
  • Floppy Drives:
    • Replacement 1.44MB floppy drives (pulled from other electronics or NOS) can be found.
    • Some generic PC floppy drives can be adapted to fit (with basic modding).
  • Floppy Drive Belts:
    • Aftermarket drive belts (generic) are available and often needed for repair.
  • Memory Sticks:
    • Original Sony Memory Sticks (up to 128MB) and some aftermarket equivalents are available.
  • CD Drives (for CD Mavicas):
    • Rare, but sometimes found as parts from donor cameras.
  • LCD Screens:
    • Sometimes available as pulls from dead cameras or via international sellers.
  • Lens Assemblies, Button Boards, etc.:
    • Usually only available as “pulls” (used parts from other non-working cameras), not new or aftermarket.

3. Aftermarket vs. Original

  • Batteries/chargers: Aftermarket is common and generally reliable, but capacity may not match originals.
  • Floppy Drives: Aftermarket (new) is rare; most are pulls from other devices. Some PC drives can be modified to work.
  • Belts and Screws: Generic belts (for tape decks, etc.) often fit; screws can be matched at hardware stores.
  • LCDs, Lens, Boards: No true aftermarket; only salvaged from other units.

4. Tips for Sourcing Parts

  • Search for “parts only” or “for repair” cameras on eBay to use as donors.
  • Cross-check part numbers (especially for LCDs and boards).
  • Be wary of “untested” parts—ask sellers for close-up photos.
  • Join forums (like Photrio, r/camerarepair on Reddit) where users sometimes trade or sell parts.
