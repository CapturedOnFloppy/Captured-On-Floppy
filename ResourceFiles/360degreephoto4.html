Captured On Floppy Tip #4 — Fast 360 Pic Fun
Many Mavica users have taken 360 degree view images using their cameras. However, this usually takes a long time. I have come up with another method for taking 360 view pictures in less than 5 seconds. Although the accuracy isn't the same as placing the camera on the tripod, and taking pictures at 30 degree intervals, my method still works well.

I tried this method on my FD-71, so I'm not sure how it will work on other cameras (I don't think it'll work on the 51) but it worked fine for me. Start by zooming the camera out ALMOST fully. Avoid a full zoom out because it will skew your pictures slightly. Now, set the rec mode to multi. Here's where the fun comes in. You'll need to spin around 360 degrees in about 5 seconds. Just click the shutter button once, then turn around. Try to keep the camera realitivly still by holding it against your chest. Now, switch the camera to playmode, and watch the shots you just took. If you turned around too slowly, you will not get a full 360 degree view, and if you turned too quickly. the images will not overlap as they should. It takes some practice, but it's a very fast way of gathering the necessary shots.

Now, open the raw image in an image editor of choice, and you will see seveveral images put together in a single image. You will now need to cut the individual images out, and place them end to end in order. You can also use these images in a slideshow format to produce an animation of your 360 view. Have fun!

There is also a Gif imager here https://www.cyberdragon.digital/apps/multimode/
