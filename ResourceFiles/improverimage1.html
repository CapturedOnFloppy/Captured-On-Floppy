Tip #1 Improving Images

I have been an avid Picassa and old Paintshop user for over 18 years now, and have applied some of my knowledge into digital photography. As all Mavica owners know, the raw image produced by the camera is a bit grainy (as with most digital cameras) Traditionally, mavica users have had to shrink the image to gain clarity. However, I have found a technique that will greatly improve the image quality without having to shrink the image. Although this tutorial is geared toward photoshop users, it will also work similar image editing programs.

Step 1
Traditionally, Mavica users start by decreasing the size of the image. However, in this technique, we will start by increasing the size of the image. Open the image you wish to edit, and select the entire image. Copy it to the clipboard, then open a new window twice the dimensions of the current graphic. Now, paste the image into the new window, and enlarge the picture until it takes up the entire new window.

Step 2
Now that you have an image twice as large as the original, we will shrink it back down to the original size (typically 640 x 480). Copy the 640 x 480 pixel picture (or whatever the dimensions you are using) to the clipboard, and close the 2x window. Paste it back into the original window. This scaling exercise will clear up some of the jaggies produced by jpeg compression. However, a bit of graininess may still remain.

Step 3
To clear up the grain, darken the image slightly. (It sometimes helps to take the picture one or two EV settings higher than usual) Now, the image should be rather clear, and have reduced jaggies. The following step will also take care of jaggie problems if they still remain.

Step 4
Finally, we will go though a series of blur/sharpen routines to clear up any remaining jaggies, or grain. Blur the picture slightly, then sharpen it. Do this once or twice, or until you achieve the results you like. By the end of this step, the image should be much more crisp and clear, and the scars of JPEG compression will be less obvious. Also, make sure you take pictures in FINE mode. This uses only 25% JPEG compression, which adds to better image quality.
