For over 35 years, I’ve lived and worked as both a professional and amateur photographer, my journey beginning in the late 1970s with studies in photography, television production, and computer programming. The first time I held a Sony Mavica, the satisfying click of the shutter and the unmistakable whirr of the floppy drive instantly transported me back to the excitement of discovering new technology as a kid. These cameras as well as other I own have become companions on many adventures, capturing moments both big and small with a charm that modern devices can't replicate.

I created Captured On Floppy as a personal tribute and resource for anyone curious about the world of Sony Mavica cameras. Building this site in old-school HTML feels like a return to the web’s early days—just as the Mavica brings me back to a simpler time in photography. As I grow older, my hope is that others will find inspiration, nostalgia, and maybe even their own treasured moments here. Whether you’re a fellow enthusiast or just discovering these quirky cameras, I invite you to explore, share, and enjoy the legacy of the Mavica.
Just and old schoool programmer, and photographer wanting to leave small legacy for Mavica owners.
Site is HTML to match most coding at the time Mavicas were produced.

About The Site : 10/10/2025
How was the site built, the project was originally on SiteJet, I wanted more authenticity so before it was completed I switched to HTML. And I copied work I did to this site. So is the site 100% HTML. Yes. However some coding was moved from sitejet and two logos were made using a free AI. That has since been removed. All photos of cameras are real cameras I edited in Picassa, and paintshop trying to stay old school. All site HTML was designed in Komposer a free oldschool HTML program.  I used Google and there AI to do research on the material I could not find.  However AI did not create the site. Remnence of the old sitejet is is in some parts of the code and would take a month to go back and remove.
So in all fairness the site is 95% true HTML with a small remenence from when I had MavicaCameras.com as the main site.
This site will only grow as I add more resources. Check out my other links as they too grow.
The site took hundreds of hours of research and coding. I go by This Old Mavica, and CapturedOnFloppy in discussion forums.
This site was made with HTML, and AI did not create this site.
Thank You and enjoy.
