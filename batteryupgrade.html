Yes, Your Mavica Should Know the Time! (Replacing the clock battery on late-model FD Mavicas)

Contribution by ExceedinglyGayDerg (Reddit)

All Mavicas allow you to set the time and date, which populate the "date created" metadata and timestamp your photos. Recent FD models (like the FD-92) may lose time when the main battery is removed—due to a backup coin cell battery. Some models use a disposable CR2025 coin cell, others (FD-92, FD87, FD97, FD100, FD200) use a rechargeable MS621FE soldered to the board.

What you'll need:

  • The replacement battery: MS621FE lithium cell
  • Soldering iron, flux, solder
  • Desoldering tool (wick or sucker)
  • #0 or #00 Philips screwdriver

Quick Steps:
  1. Open the camera (remove main battery first, unscrew all outside screws)
  2. Disconnect ribbon cables and display/control PCB; locate old battery
  3. Desolder and remove the old battery
  4. Prep and fit the new MS621FE battery into place
  5. Solder new battery, clean up, reassemble camera
  6. Take your time reassembling, mind the ribbon cable locks

Model notes: The tutorial is for FD-92; similar applies to FD87, FD97, FD100, FD200 (rechargeable battery). FD83, FD88, FD85, FD90, FD95 use the same battery, but it's surface-mount rather than through-hole.
