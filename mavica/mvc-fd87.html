Sony Mavica MVC-FD87: Full Details

Historical Context

  • Release Date: 2001
  • Lineage: The FD87 was part of the last generation of floppy-disk Mavicas, introduced alongside the FD85 and FD92. These models represent Sony’s final push to bridge the gap between floppy disk and solid-state memory (Memory Stick).
  • Market Position: Targeted at advanced users, educators, and business professionals who needed both higher resolution and longer zoom, with the convenience of universal storage.

Detailed Features

1. Storage & Transfer

  • Floppy Disk: Maintained Sony’s tradition of instant compatibility with virtually any PC.
  • Memory Stick Support: Via the MSAC-FD2M adapter, enabling higher capacity and faster storage (images and movies).
  • Disk Management: Floppies for quick sharing, Memory Stick for extended shoots.

2. Image Quality & Sensor

  • 1.3 Megapixels: 1280 x 960 resolution, suitable for quality prints up to 5x7" or even 8x10" with careful processing.
  • JPEG Compression: "Fine" and "Standard" settings; limited capacity per floppy at full quality.
  • BMP Option: For uncompressed images (few per disk).


3. Lens & Zoom

  • 6x Optical Zoom: 41–246mm (35mm equivalent), versatile for portraits, events, and moderate telephoto needs.
  • Aperture: f/2.8 (wide) to f/3.0 (tele), decent low-light performance.
  • Macro Mode: For sharp close-ups (approx. 5cm).

4. Display & Controls

  • LCD: 2.5” color screen for composing and playback.
  • Electronic Viewfinder: Useful outdoors and for conserving battery.
  • Menu System: Icon-driven, with settings for white balance, exposure, and media management.
  • Playback: Review, delete, and protect images directly on camera.

5. Battery & Power

  • Info Lithium Batteries: Long-lasting, shared across Sony camcorders and cameras.
  • AC Adapter: Optional for continuous indoor use.

6. Flash & Output

  • Pop-up Flash: Automatic, with red-eye reduction.
  • Video Out: NTSC/PAL for TV or projector viewing.
  • Multi-Terminal: For accessories and data.

7. Additional Features

  • Self Timer: 10s for group/self-portraits.
  • White Balance: Auto, Indoor, Outdoor for color accuracy.
  • Exposure Compensation: +/- 1.5 EV in 0.5-step increments.
  • Movie Mode: Short MPEG-EX movies (with Memory Stick only).

User Experience

  • Image Quality: Considerably improved over VGA models, good for home, office, and educational use.
  • Versatility: 6x zoom was a compromise between the compact 3x and the massive 14x of the FD88.
  • Portability: Still bulky due to the floppy and zoom lens, but practical for most non-pocket uses.
  • Speed: Floppy write times were slow, but Memory Stick enabled faster shooting and larger capacity.
  • Reliability: Robust build; disks and adapters required careful handling.

Legacy & Impact

  • Hybrid Storage: The FD87’s support for both floppy disks and Memory Stick made it a true transitional device, bridging the gap between the floppy era and the emerging memory card age.
  • Mainstream Appeal: Popular in schools, offices, and among families for its flexibility and universal compatibility.
  • Collector Value: Highly regarded for its versatility and as one of the last "floppy Mavicas."

Comparison to Other Mavica Models

  • FD85: 1.3MP, 3x zoom; FD87 offers a longer 6x zoom.
  • FD88: 1.2MP, massive 14x zoom, floppy-only (no Memory Stick).
  • FD92: 2.1MP, 8x zoom, Memory Stick support; higher end and higher resolution.
  • FD95/FD200: Successors with even higher resolution and dedicated Memory Stick or CD-R storage.

Trivia & Notes

  • The FD87 was among the last major floppy disk digital cameras ever sold, as the industry rapidly shifted to solid-state memory.
  • The included Memory Stick Floppy Disk Adapter (MSAC-FD2M) became a popular accessory for later Sony cameras as well.
  • Today, the FD87 is prized by collectors for its dual-media flexibility and as a capstone of the classic Mavica line.
