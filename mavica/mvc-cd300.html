Sony Mavica MVC-FD300: Full Details

Historical Context

  • Release Date: 2002
  • Lineage: The FD300 was one of the final hybrid (floppy + Memory Stick) Mavica cameras, following the FD200 and arriving alongside the last wave of Sony’s floppy-based digital cameras.
  • Market Position: Targeted at home users, educators, and businesses needing simple, PC-compatible image transfer with the option of higher-capacity Memory Stick storage.

Key Features & Specifications

Feature Details

Resolution 1600 x 1200 pixels (JPEG)
Lens 3x optical zoom, 6.3–19mm f/2.8–2.9 (41–123mm equiv.)

Release Price ~$350 USD


Detailed Features

1. Sensor & Image Quality

  • 2MP resolution: 1600 x 1200 for prints up to 8x10".
  • JPEG compression with Fine/Standard quality and BMP support.

2. Lens & Zoom

  • 3x optical zoom (41–123mm equiv.), suitable for most daily shooting.
  • Macro mode for close-ups.

3. Storage & Transfer

  • Hybrid storage: uses both 3.5" floppies and Memory Stick (no adapter needed).
  • Floppy: universal compatibility, but limited capacity.
  • Memory Stick: higher capacity and enables video recording.

4. Display & Controls

  • 2.5" LCD for shooting/reviewing images.
  • Simple menu system for exposure, WB, and playback.

5. Battery & Power

  • InfoLithium batteries for reliable, long-lasting use.
  • Optional AC adapter for extended sessions.

6. Flash & Output

  • Pop-up flash with auto mode and red-eye reduction.
  • AV out for TV viewing.

7. Other Features

  • Self timer, exposure compensation, USB for file transfer.

User Experience

  • Image Quality: Decent for its era; good for documentation and web use.
  • Storage Flexibility: Hybrid system let users shoot on floppy for quick sharing or MS for higher capacity and video.
  • Speed: Floppy write times slow; Memory Stick much faster.
  • Portability: Compact and lightweight for a Mavica; easy to carry.
  • Reliability: Floppies and Memory Sticks both robust with careful handling.

Legacy & Impact

  • Hybrid Bridge: Helped late adopters transition from floppy to solid-state storage.
  • End of an Era: One of the last Sony floppy-based digital cameras.
  • Collector Value: Sought after by collectors for its hybrid features and as a “last of its kind.”

Comparison to Other Mavica Models

  • FD200: Successor, similar body/features; 3x zoom, improved MPEG video capability.
  • FD95/FD97: 2.1MP, 10x zoom, larger, more advanced; hybrid storage.
  • FD92: 2.1MP, 8x zoom, hybrid, earlier model.
  • FD87/FD85: 1.3MP, 6x/3x zoom, Memory Stick via adapter only.
  • FD73/FD88: VGA/1.2MP, lower res, more shots per floppy, floppy-only.

Trivia & Notes

  • The FD100, FD200, and FD300 are sometimes considered the “last floppy Mavicas.”
  • MPEG movie mode only available with Memory Stick.
  • Hybrid design and 2MP sensor made it a practical choice for late digital adopte

My Opinion:

The Sony Mavica CD300 marked a significant step forward in the Mavica CD line, blending the familiar convenience of direct-to-CD storage with a leap in image quality thanks to its 3.3-megapixel CCD sensor. Unlike earlier Mavicas which prioritized workflow over outright photographic performance, the CD300 was designed for users who demanded higher resolution and color fidelity without sacrificing the unique draw of mini CD-R archiving. Its 3x optical zoom lens delivered sharp, vibrant images, and the camera’s improved electronics resulted in more natural color rendering and lower noise—making it a practical tool for everything from travel snapshots to documentation, and even creative projects.

Where the CD300 truly excelled was in delivering the best of both worlds: it provided all the archival and sharing advantages of the Mavica CD system—hundreds of images per disc, immediate compatibility with most computers, and reliable long-term storage—while also satisfying the needs of more discerning photographers. The camera’s user interface was refined, the autofocus was responsive, and video capture was improved. For educators, small businesses, and families, the CD300 was both a capable everyday camera and an innovative digital archive solution. Today, it’s remembered as one of the most well-balanced and advanced Mavicas, representing the peak of the series before the digital camera market fully shifted to memory card-based storage. Things became very real for the technology at this point. However the CD idea why amazing would not last as long or be as popular as the Floppies were.
