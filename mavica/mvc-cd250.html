Sony Mavica MVC-CD250

About the CD250:
The Sony Mavica CD250 is a compact, consumer-focused continuation of Sony's CD-based Mavica line. It offered straightforward CD archiving (8cm CD-R/RW), a 2.0MP CCD, and a 3x optical zoom in a simplified, approachable package for everyday users. ( do not own these images are stock created by me)

Historical Context

  • Release Date: 2002
  • Lineage: Released alongside the CD200; largely identical with small regional/firmware differences.
  • Market Position: Entry to mid-level consumer camera — aimed at families, schools and casual shooters.

Detailed Features

Sensor & Image Quality

2MP CCD — good for family snapshots and web use; JPEG capture with reasonable color and detail for the era.

Lens & Zoom

3x optical zoom covering most everyday situations; macro mode available for close-ups.

Storage & Transfer

Direct-to-disc CD storage (156MB) — high capacity compared to floppies; rewritable CD support allowed reuse and easy archiving.

Display & Controls

2.5" LCD-only operation, simple icon-driven menus for easy use.

Other

Pop-up flash, self-timer, exposure compensation and USB file transfer.

User Experience

  • Image Quality: Good for family and event photography.
  • Storage: CD media allowed many photos per disc — great for single-session archiving.
  • Speed: CD writing is slower than flash, but fine for deliberate archiving workflows.
  • Portability: Compact and easy to handle for a CD camera.
  • Reliability: CDs are durable when cared for; avoid scratches and fingerprints on discs.

Legacy & Impact

  • Mainstream CD: Helped bring optical-disc archiving to everyday users.
  • End of an Era: One of the practical, consumer-friendly CD Mavicas before flash-memory cameras dominated.
  • Collector Value: Representative of the CD Mavica era — appreciated by enthusiasts.

Comparison to Other Mavica Models

  • CD200: Twin model, nearly identical.
  • CD1000: Earlier, larger model with more zoom.
  • CD350: Newer model with higher resolution.

My Opinion:

The Sony Mavica CD250 represents a practical, user-focused refinement of Sony's optical-media cameras. With a reliable 2.0MP sensor, 3x zoom and direct-to-CD archiving, it made digital image sharing and long-term backups approachable for schools, families and event shooters. It wasn’t cutting-edge in specs, but its simplicity and the “burn-and-hand-over” workflow were genuinely useful at the time — a neat, pragmatic step in the Mavica story.
